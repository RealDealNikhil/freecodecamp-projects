Aaron Copland

The Dean of American Composers

Here's a timeline of Aaron Copland's life:

  • 1900 - Born on Nov. 14th in Brooklyn, NY.
  • 1920 - Copland decides to continue his musical studies in Fontainebleau, France, where he receives tutelage from the famed Nadia Boulanger.
  • 1925 - Debuts Symphony for Organ and Orchestra on Jan. 11 with the New York Symphony Society under Walter Damrosch.
  • 1930 - Piano Variations
  • 1935 - El Salon Mexico
  • 1939 - Composes the score to Of Mice and Men.
  • 1942 - Fanfare for the Common Man
  • 1944 - Composes the music for Martha Graham's dance Appalachian Spring.
  • 1945 - Copland wins the Pulitzer Prize for his work on Appalachian Spring.
  • 1949 - Composes the score to The Heiress (for which he eventually wins an Oscar).
  • 1964 - Presented with the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Lyndon Johnson on Sept. 14.
  • 1970s - Copland no longer works on creating new works and instead begins to focus on teaching and conducting.
  • 1986 - Awarded the National Medal of Arts.
  • 1990 - Copland dies at the age of 90.
  • 1999 - Howard Pollack publishes the well received biography: Aaron Copland: The Life & Work of an Uncommon Man.
